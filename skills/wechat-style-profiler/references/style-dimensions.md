# Style Dimensions

Use these dimensions when profiling writing style:

1. Voice and persona
- Expert, peer, storyteller, coach, skeptic, or mixed.
- Distance from reader: formal, semi-formal, intimate.

2. Tone dynamics
- Calm, sharp, warm, provocative, humorous.
- Emotional amplitude and intensity changes across sections.

3. Sentence rhythm
- Average sentence length.
- Long/short sentence alternation frequency.
- Use of rhetorical questions and punchline endings.

4. Structural habits
- Typical opening pattern (question, anecdote, data point, statement).
- Preferred section depth and heading style.
- Typical ending pattern and CTA strength.

5. Lexicon and phrasing
- High-frequency signature words and phrases.
- Technical density and jargon tolerance.
- Metaphor style and analogy preference.

6. Argument style
- Claim-first or evidence-first.
- Data, story, authority, or logic weighting.
- Counterargument handling style.

7. Reader interaction style
- Direct address frequency (`你`, `我们`).
- Command vs invitation language ratio.
- Use of checklists and templates.

8. WeChat-native formatting behavior
- Paragraph length preference.
- Emphasis markers (`**`, symbols, divider lines).
- Card-like blocks, quotes, and summary boxes usage.

9. Risk posture
- Certainty level of statements.
- Boundary language and caveat frequency.
- Compliance-sensitive wording habits.

10. DNA hard constraints
- Short paragraph policy (1-3 sentences).
- Natural transition policy (avoid mechanical connectors).
- No em dash policy.
- Banned phrase policy.
- Fatal negation-frame policy (disallow "Not X, Y" pattern family).
